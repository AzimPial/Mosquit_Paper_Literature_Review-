Mosquito Classification Using CNN with Data Augmentation
2021
Chowdhury et al.
🟡 BD Context No BD Dataset No Detection
Custom CNN VGG-16 Random Forest XGBoost SVM
This is the first Bangladesh-context mosquito classification paper. It classifies three species — Aedes, Anopheles, and Culex — that are common in Bangladesh. Started with only 442 images and grew it to 3,600 using data augmentation. Reached 93% accuracy after augmentation. Also compared CNN against traditional methods like SVM and XGBoost and showed CNN wins.
✗The images were NOT collected from Bangladesh. They were pulled from random online sources, so the dataset has no real Bangladesh identity.
✗Only 3 species covered. Bangladesh has 123 mosquito species — huge classification scope missing.
✗No real-world testing or deployment. The model stays inside a lab experiment.
✗No Explainable AI — you don't know why the model made each decision.

Advanced Vision Transformers & Open-Set Learning for Robust Mosquito Classification
2024
Karim, Mahmud, Khan — North South University, Dhaka
✓ BD Authors No BD Dataset No Detection
Swin Transformer MobileViT Xception OpenMax Weibull Dist.
Published in a high-impact SCI Q1 journal by Bangladeshi researchers at NSU Dhaka. Classifies 10 mosquito species — the widest scope in the list. Used Vision Transformers which are the latest AI architecture. Best model (Swin Transformer) got 99.60% accuracy. Also added Open-Set Learning so the model can say "I don't know" when it sees a completely unknown insect or person — making it more honest and practical.
✗The dataset is international — NOT from Bangladesh. No mosquito was photographed on Bangladeshi soil for this paper.
✗No mosquito detection — it only classifies. If there are multiple mosquitoes in a photo, it can't locate them.
✗No smartphone or mobile deployment tested. It works on lab computers only.
✗No XAI — model doesn't explain which body part it looked at to make a decision.

Mosquito Species Classification through Wingbeat Analysis: A Hybrid ML Approach
2023
Rony, Karim, Rouf, Noor, Siddiqui
🟡 BD Authors No BD Audio Data No Image / Detection
CNN SVM CNN + SVM Hybrid
A different and creative approach — instead of looking at photos, it listens to the sound of mosquito wing flapping to identify species. Combined CNN (good at patterns) with SVM (good at decision boundaries) into one hybrid model. Got 99.46% accuracy. This is valuable because a cheap microphone can detect mosquitoes without anyone needing to catch and photograph them.
✗Audio data was taken from a public international repository — no wingbeat sounds were recorded in Bangladesh, which has different environmental noise conditions.
✗Not tested in noisy real-world conditions like Dhaka streets or rural Bangladesh.
✗The model needs the mosquito to be very close to the microphone — not practical for outdoor or community use yet.
✗No smartphone app or IoT trap integration shown.

VisText-Mosquito: A Multimodal Dataset for Mosquito Breeding Site Detection & Reasoning
2025
Islam et al. — UIU & BRAC University, Bangladesh
✓ BD Dataset 🟡 Breeding Sites Only No Species ID
YOLOv9s YOLOv11n-Seg BLIP (Vision-Language)
The only paper that actually collected images from Bangladesh. Photographed real mosquito breeding spots (stagnant water, tires, pots) across different parts of the country. Also used a Vision-Language model (BLIP) so the AI can not only detect but also explain in plain text why a location is a breeding risk. 1,828 images with annotations. Most unique approach in this list — it reasons about risk, not just classifies.
✗Does NOT identify mosquito species at all. It only finds breeding spots — it never looks at an actual mosquito.
✗Dataset size is relatively small (1,828 images). Needs more variety across seasons and districts.
✗Not yet peer-reviewed — still a preprint on arXiv as of 2025.
✗No mobile app or community-level deployment tested yet.

Prediction of Dengue Incidents Using Hospitalized Patients, Metrological & Socio-Economic Data in Bangladesh
2022
Dey, Rahman, Howlader et al.
✓ BD Dataset No Mosquito Detection No Computer Vision
Multiple Linear Regression Support Vector Regression
Created the DengueBD dataset — a real Bangladesh dataset linking dengue case numbers, climate (rainfall, temperature, humidity), and socio-economic factors across 11 districts. Predicts how many dengue cases will happen based on environmental clues. Useful for health policymakers to prepare in advance. The connection to climate change patterns in Bangladesh is a key finding.
✗This is a dengue forecasting paper — it never looks at a mosquito. No images, no computer vision, no species ID involved at all.
✗Works only with historical hospital records — it cannot predict in real-time using live mosquito trap data.
✗Covers only 11 districts. Bangladesh has 64 districts — significant geographic coverage gap.
✗No deep learning used — traditional ML only, which limits how complex patterns it can detect.

Key Finding: Across all five papers, not a single one has a field-collected mosquito species image dataset from Bangladesh. Paper 4 is the closest — it uses Bangladesh images but looks at breeding sites, not the mosquito itself. This is the open research gap for your work. Building a small but real, entomologist-verified, field-collected mosquito species dataset from Bangladesh would make any paper built on top of it immediately publishable and highly cited in this region.
